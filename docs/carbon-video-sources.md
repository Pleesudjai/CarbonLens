# Carbon Video Sources

This note documents the figures used in the short CarbonLens construction-carbon graphic.

## Stats used in the video

### 1. Buildings and construction

- `34% of global CO2 emissions`
- Source:
  - UNEP / GlobalABC, *Global Status Report for Buildings and Construction 2024-2025: Key Messages*
- Repository page:
  - https://wedocs.unep.org/handle/20.500.11822/47261

Relevant wording from the UNEP repository abstract:
- buildings account for `32%` of global energy use
- buildings contribute `34%` of global CO2 emissions

### 2. Cement as the main concrete carbon driver

- Video figure used: `~2.5 Gt CO2 direct cement emissions in 2022`
- This value is an inference from IEA data:
  - direct cement emissions intensity in 2022: `just under 0.6 t CO2 per tonne of cement`
  - global cement production in 2022: `4 158 Mt`
- Approximate calculation:
  - `0.6 x 4,158 Mt = 2,495 Mt CO2`
  - `= ~2.5 Gt CO2`

Source page:
- IEA, *Cement*
- https://www.iea.org/energy-system/industry/cement

## Message the video is supporting

The graphic is intentionally simple:

- construction-sector carbon is globally significant
- concrete carbon is heavily driven by cement
- CarbonLens should compare both:
  - `Material Carbon`
  - `During-Build Carbon`
  - not just one or the other
