# Top 5 Actions Before Judging

Updated: 2026-04-04

Project: `CarbonLens / GreenRoute Transit`

Goal:
- Turn the current strong prototype into a strong `judge experience`
- Focus on the highest-value moves left before final submission

## 1. Lock The Main Carbon Story

Use one simple line throughout the pitch and demo:

`FRC is not only a lower-embodied-carbon section. It also cuts construction time, which reduces traffic-delay, detour, and equipment emissions.`

What to do:
- say `total carbon` when you mean `embodied + construction-phase`
- say `embodied carbon` when you mean materials only
- say `build penalty score` when you mean the 1-10 constructability penalty
- avoid mixing those terms live

Why it matters:
- this is now the strongest technical differentiator in the project
- it is also the easiest thing for judges to misunderstand if phrased loosely

## 2. Rehearse A Tight 2-Minute Demo

Recommended flow:
1. show Phoenix and the existing Valley Metro network
2. turn on `Road CO2 Pressure` or `Mode-Shift Opportunity`
3. compare the corridor alternatives
4. open the results and stacked carbon chart
5. point to the FRC section insight and the total-carbon difference
6. end on the recommendation

What to avoid:
- spending too long inside edit controls
- over-explaining every metric
- switching overlays too many times

Why it matters:
- the product is already rich enough
- the win now comes from clarity and pacing

## 3. Prepare A Reliable Demo Backup

What to prepare:
- one hosted demo if possible
- one local backup
- 4-6 backup screenshots
- one short backup screen recording

Why it matters:
- this repo has already had local proxy/dev-server hiccups
- judges remember smooth demos more than perfect local architecture

## 4. Add One Headline Impact Claim

Pick one memorable claim and repeat it.

Good direction:
- `The FRC option lowers total carbon, not just embodied carbon, because faster construction cuts traffic-delay emissions.`
- `This tool helps communities compare where rail can remove more driving and where it costs less carbon to build.`

Why it matters:
- the demo already shows many metrics
- judges still want one sentence they can remember later

## 5. Finish Submission Assets

Minimum set:
- final pitch deck
- final submission writeup
- final team/speaking roles
- final demo script
- short limitations note

Include this limitations language clearly:
- conceptual planning tool
- transparent public-data proxies
- not final stamped design
- not exact operational CO2 measurement

Why it matters:
- the product is already ahead of many hackathon projects
- incomplete submission materials are now a bigger risk than missing features

## Bottom Line

The hardest work is already done.

The next win is not another feature. The next win is making the existing product feel:
- clear
- credible
- memorable
- reliable in front of judges
